Kraken Boss Showcase









Prototype I made using Unreal Engine. For this project I attempted to replicate a souls-like gameplay where the character runs, rolls, and attacks. As an enemy I used the kraken asset that can be found in the asset marketplace. I implemented the idle, walk, attacking animation for it(which has an explosion effect on the impact frame, but tampered with it to get that dust effect shown in the video), and made it into a patrolling AI that attacks the player once it sees them. A healthbar and death animation were also implemented into the kraken.

Sequencer Showcase









Quick Star Wars scene I made using the Unreal Engine sequencer. Modeled the hangar inside the engine itself, tweaked with the material to make the pattering a bit more random. The models and ships are from Sketchfab and the walk animation from Mixamo. In the scene there's three camera angles. The first one shows them from behind as well as some tie fighters passing by. Second camera is from the front distancing itself to show the scale of the numbers. Last angle is the two troopers about to get on the ship.

Horror Sequencer









Scene made with Unreal Engine Sequencer. Made it based on a weird sleep paralysis experience I had once. For this one I had made the walls first to make the room in, and then had started implementing the props and character models. For the sleep paralysis demon I combined crouching, running, and diving animations to make the jumpscare. Also, implemented sounds that sounded very similar to what I heard that night. Was probably half asleep or maybe dreamed that, but it sure terrified me.

Final Buzz









In this project I focused on the programing of enemies and player movement. It was done in Unity which uses C# as its default programming language. The boss being showcased is a giant wasp shooting stingers at the player. The player is able to jump on the stingers and bounce off of them.

Grey boxing project









Project that was worked on with classmates where we had to do a greyboxing of a game idea to get the feel of what a prototype starts like. This short clip is showcasing the wallrunning and shooting that was gonna be implemented in the final idea. Our idea was heavely inspired by shooter games like "Titan Fall" where you can essentially do similar kind of wallrunning. The prototype was done in Unity, programmed via C#.

Enemy Behaviour









AI project showcasing different behaviours such as: Patrol, Chase, Proximity, Random, and combinations of them as well. This was made in Unity with C#. The green capsules are the ones that do the chasing and the red ones are the ones that get chased.
